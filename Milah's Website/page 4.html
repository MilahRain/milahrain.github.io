My Experience Coding With C++

C++ was the second programming language I learned, right after Java, and it opened up an entirely new way of thinking about programming. While Java introduced me to structure and object-oriented logic, C++ pushed me closer to the hardware and helped me understand how programs actually manage memory, data, and performance behind the scenes. Taking both Level 1 and Level 2 courses gave me a strong foundation not only in the basics, but also in more advanced concepts that challenged me to think more critically and independently.

One of the things I appreciated most about C++ was how much control it gives you. Working with pointers, references, file I/O, and dynamic memory allocation taught me the importance of responsibility in programming, every decision you make has consequences. Even though these topics were difficult at times, I enjoyed the sense of precision and intentionality that C++ demands. I found that I was particularly good at breaking down logic step-by-step and debugging carefully, especially when working on multi-file projects or programs that relied heavily on user input.

Of course, C++ isnâ€™t always easy. Managing memory manually, handling segmentation faults, and keeping track of object lifecycles can be frustrating, especially when transitioning from languages that take care of those details for you. But those challenges were also some of the most rewarding parts of learning C++. They taught me patience, attention to detail, and the value of writing clean, efficient code.

Overall, learning C++ strengthened my confidence as a programmer. It helped bridge the gap between high-level problem solving and low-level understanding, and it shaped the way I think about efficiency, structure, and control in my programs. The skills I developed in both C++ courses continue to support me in every new language I learn and every project I take on.
